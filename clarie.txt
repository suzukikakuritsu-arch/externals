## 1. Foundation: IPS Non-Negativity

The Information Probability Symmetry (IPS) principle states:

    I = P × S ≥ 0

Information is non-negative by definition.
This induces a non-negative action integral over the zeta function.

## 2. Action Integral

Define the action:

    S(σ) = ∫_{-∞}^{∞} |ζ(σ + it)|² dt

By IPS non-negativity:

    S(σ) ≥ 0  for all σ

A non-trivial zero s₀ satisfies ζ(s₀) = 0, which is a minimum of S(σ).

## 3. Functional Equation Symmetry

The Riemann zeta function satisfies:

    ζ(s) = ζ(1-s) × F(s),  where |F(s)| = 1 on the critical line

This induces symmetry in the action:

    S(σ) = S(1-σ)

The unique axis of symmetry is σ = 1/2.

## 4. Variational Condition

Applying the variational principle δS = 0:

    dS/dσ = 0

Combined with S(σ) = S(1-σ), the unique critical point is:

    σ = 1/2

No other critical point exists consistent with this symmetry.

## 5. Elimination of 4-Pattern

Candidate zero configurations:

    Pattern-2 (valid):   s,  1-s          → symmetric under σ = 1/2  ✓
    Pattern-4 (invalid): s,  1-s, -s, 1+s → requires additional symmetry axes ✗

Pattern-4 would require S(σ) to have critical points at σ ≠ 1/2.
This contradicts the unique symmetry axis derived in Step 3.
Therefore Pattern-4 is eliminated.

## 6. Correspondence with Theorem H1

H1 (Fibonacci-KS discrepancy) exhibits the same 2-pattern structure:

    Even k:  D(F_k) = φ^{-(k+1)}        ↔  s
    Odd  k:  D(F_k) = 1 - φ^{-(k+1)}    ↔  1-s

The even/odd duality of H1 is the discrete analogue of the
s ↔ 1-s symmetry in the Riemann functional equation.

φ-attractor in SUD dynamics produces exactly 2 stable states,
not 4, consistent with the variational minimum at σ = 1/2.
